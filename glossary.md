# Glossary

This is a glossary for any terms used in this dashboard.

## Calculated Metrics Definitions

1. Close Rate- *Sale Count* / *Quote Count*. The % of quotes that eventually convert into a policy.
2. Quote Count- Count of unique *Quote ID*. A count of all quotes.
3. Sale Count- Count of unique *Quote ID* where *Issued Indicator* is "Y". A count of all quotes that eventually converted into a policy.

## Field Definitions

1. Quote ID- Unique identifier for each quote
2. Agency Name- Name of agent where quote was provided
3. Issued Indicator- "Y" means policy was issued (bound). "N" means policy was not issued.
4. Quote Platform- Platform used by agent to get the quote (i.e. comparative rater or company portal). eQuote is the company portal. The others are comparative raters.
5. Effective Date- Effective date of the quoted policy
6. Liability Only Indicator- "Y" means quote is only for liability coverages (Bodily Injury & Property Damage). "N" means quote is for full coverage (Liability + Physical Damage).
7. State- Risk state
8. Zip Code- Risk Zip Code
9. Territory- Risk Territory
10. Minimum Vehicle Year- Oldest vehicle being quoted (model year)
11. Maximum Vehicle Year- Newest vehicle being quotes (model year)
12. Multi-Product Discount Indicator- "Auto Home" means customer either already has a Homeowners policy or is being quoted a Homeowners policy. "Homeowner" means customer owns a home but that home is not insured (or being quoted) with carrier. "None" means customer is not a homeowner
13. Life Discount Indicator- "Y" means customer either already has a Life policy or is being quoted a Life policy. "N" means customer does not have (or being quoted) a Life policy
14. Multi-Car Discount Indicator- "Y" means 2 or more vehicles are being quoted. "N" means 1 vehicle is on the quote
15. Accident/Violation Score- A score that looks at the number and combination of accidents and violations on the quoted drivers' MVR
0 = no accidents or violations.
16. Insurance Score- A score based on financial information similar to a credit score. Higher scores are designed to be better risks
17. CSL/BI Limit- Bodily injury limits being quoted on the policy
18. Driver 1 Age- Age of the first named driver
19. Driver 2 Age- Age of the second named driver
